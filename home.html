Ariel Jonathan Roffé

Intro

Hi! I'm Ariel Roffé. I'm a philosophy reasearcher from Argentina. Blablabla.

My research areas are the logic of science and the philosophy of biology (especially of systematics). I also develop computational applications for philosophers. I received my PhD from the University of Buenos Aires in 2020, I have a research scholarship from CONICET, and a teaching position at the Department of Philosophy of the University of Buenos Aires.

I am a member of the Grupo ANFIBIO, the Centro de Filosofía e Historia de la Ciencia and the BA-Logic Group, as well as other research groups in the philosophy of science and in mathematical logic.

Software

TAUT

TAUT is a website where you can find a variety of randomly-generated, self-correcting logic excercises.

You can access it via: https://www.taut-logic.com/

See also:

  • Daily Nous post
  • CONICET article (in spanish)

logics

Logics is a Python framework for mathematical logic. It aims at generality (being able to represent as many systems as possible), well-documented and readable code and minimal dependencies, rather than speed. Some of its intended applications are educational software (e.g. TAUT uses a previous version of this package), and quick prototyping of ideas for research purposes

You can access it via: https://github.com/ariroffe/logics

To see the documentation, visit: https://logics.readthedocs.io/en/latest/?

Reconstructor

Reconstructor is a program designed to test and run formal reconstructions of theories, made in the structuralist format. It also does some cool logic stuff, and I believe it can be useful for teaching logic.
